Pasta

Description

Pasta is a versatile Italian food made from a dough of wheat flour (often durum semolina), water, and sometimes eggs, formed into various shapes (like spaghetti, penne, lasagna) and typically cooked by boiling or baking, then served with sauces or fillings. It's a staple of Italian cuisine, ranging from short tubes and ribbons to stuffed varieties, and can be fresh or dried.

Ingrediants

  • 1 ½ cups rotini pasta
  • 3 tablespoons pesto, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon granulated garlic
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ cup halved grape tomatoes
  • ½ cup small (pearlini) fresh mozzarella balls
  • 2 leaves fresh basil leaves, finely shredded

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil
  2. cook the rotini at a boil until tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes; drain.
  3. Mix pesto, olive oil, salt, granulated garlic, and black pepper in a bowl;
  4. Add rotini.
  5. Toss to coat.
  6. Fold in tomatoes, mozzarella, and fresh basil.
